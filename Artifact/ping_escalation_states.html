Ping escalation model — how repeated unanswered pings change intensity and countenance from neutral to critical

ping escalation — countenance model

Ping 1 — normal

Standard vibration. Neutral mood.

Single short buzz. Receiver sees sender name + predefined message.

calm

Ping 2 — noticed

No reply to first ping. Sender re-pings.

Slightly longer buzz pattern. UI tint shifts to amber on receiver's end. Receiver knows this is a follow-up, not a first contact.

attention

Ping 3 — urgent

Still no reply. Third ping unlocks urgency tier.

Double pulse vibration. UI runs red. Overrides DND on receiver's device. This ping cannot be silently ignored — it surfaces above other notifications.

urgent

Ping 4 — critical

Horse blood red. No further pings permitted after this.

Triple pulse. Screen flashes on receiver side. Sender is locked from pinging further — protocol enforces a cooldown. The ball is entirely in the receiver's court.

critical — locked

After ping 4, the sender cannot re-ping until the receiver responds or the cooldown window expires. Prevents harassment. Preserves protocol integrity.

ping-back count — the ignored signal record

When sender is convinced the receiver has ignored — not missed — the signal, the app surfaces a visible ping-back count on the receiver's profile. A silent social record.

0 ignored → 1–2 ignored → 3–4 ignored → 5+ ignored

What triggers an "ignored" mark

Signal was delivered (double tick). Reply window elapsed. Sender manually marks as ignored — or system auto-flags after configurable window. Receiver's ping-back count increments.

Who sees the ping-back count

Only the sender sees it on their end — against that specific relationship. It is not public. It is a personal accountability record. Like a call-back log that never got returned.

What the receiver sees

Nothing surfaced to the receiver directly — but their response rate is tracked silently. Useful in professional contexts where the sender is a superior. The record exists.

The ping-back count is not punitive. It is informational. It tells the sender: this person has a pattern. Adjust your expectations or your relationship setup.
