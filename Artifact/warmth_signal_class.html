Warmth signal class — definition, orb states, comparison with action signal, and setup flow

two signal classes — side by side

Action signal

Hierarchical — one side always superior

Reply expected — got it or decline

Session governed — expiry, cooldown, escalation

History recorded — immutable log both sides

Max 4 pings — sealed at critical

Orb color — amber through crimson

Warmth signal

Peer — no hierarchy, either side initiates

Reply optional — no pressure, no debt

Sessionless — no expiry, no escalation

History soft — count only, no timestamps

One ping only — no re-ping ever

Orb color — soft purple, pink, teal

The warmth signal borrows the same delivery layer — one vibration, one Orb glow — but everything governing it is stripped back. No accountability. No urgency. It lands, it glows, it fades. That's its entire lifecycle.

warmth orb states — soft palette, gentle rhythm

SP

soft purple · slow breath · no reply needed

warm pink · gentle pulse · reassurance

soft teal · steady glow · arrival

SP

Fading out

glow dissolves · no session left open

The warmth palette deliberately avoids amber and red entirely. Those colors are owned by the action signal — urgency and consequence. Warmth lives in purple, pink, and teal — colors that feel human, not operational. The receiver's brain learns this distinction without being told.
