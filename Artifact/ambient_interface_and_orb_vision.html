Ambient interface design — orb states, zero-banner phone UI, and product phase roadmap

orb states — live animations

Each state has a biological rhythm. You perceive it, you don't read it.

idle — dark

queued — slow breath

waiting — amber glow

ping 3 — red pulse

critical — rapid crimson

got it — green flash

DND break — white jolt

The pulse frequency is the urgency signal. A slow amber breath says "something is pending, no rush." A rapid crimson strobe says "this needed you 40 minutes ago." No text. No banner. Just light and rhythm.

in-pocket response — gesture protocol

When the phone is in a pocket or face-down, the haptic is the interface. No screen needed.

Single back-tap (iPhone) or squeeze (Android)

Phone pulsing in pocket — tap the back of the device once

→ sends "got it"

Double back-tap

Registered by accelerometer / back-tap gesture API

→ sends "decline"

Haptic breath pattern

Every 2 minutes while session is active — faint rhythmic pulse so you never forget something is pending

→ passive awareness, no action required

Back-tap is a native iOS accessibility feature already. Android has squeeze gestures on Pixel devices. This means in-pocket response ships without any special hardware — just OS-level gesture mapping.
