1 — Brand: Nod → Flash

Every reference updated throughout the spec. "Flash" as noun, verb, and brand. Flash calling, flash back, flashing.

Why: "Flash" is a cultural verb already in global use. It implies speed, light, and active intent rather than passive agreement.

2 — Short-pulse call: 3 seconds → 8 seconds

The incoming flash behaves like a ringing call for exactly 8 seconds before settling into the persistent ambient Orb.

Why: 8 seconds solves notification blindness. Long enough to be unmissable. Short enough to never feel like an obligation.

3 — Binary Soldiers replace text buttons

❌ bottom-left always. ⚡ bottom-right during ringing. ✔️ bottom-right during passive Orb. No text labels on action buttons.

Why: Icons process 3× faster than text. Left-negative, right-positive locks into muscle memory after first use. Crisis-speed interaction.

4 — Submerged identity anchor (DP inside Orb)

DP appears immediately inside the Orb from first flash. Masked and submerged — not a flat photo overlay. State-aware opacity and saturation.

Why: Face recognition is faster than name reading. Identity confirmed in peripheral vision before focus lands on any text.

5 — Melt transition on soldier tap

Tapping a soldier causes it to bloom outward then collapse as the Orb transitions to its final state — green bloom for got-it, red bloom for decline.

Why: Tactile satisfaction. The transaction visibly seals. Mental state shifts from active to complete in one motion.

0 Core philosophy foundation ›

The flash is the message

A flash carries zero content. Its meaning is borrowed entirely from the pre-established relationship. The signal itself is the communication.

Zero social debt

Flash back — debt paid. Decline — debt respectfully refused. No lingering obligation. The session ends and everyone moves on.

Peripheral awareness

You perceive the state, you don't check it. Color, rhythm, and light over banners and text. The environment is the interface.

Consent is mutual

No relationship can be imposed unilaterally. Both parties agree to every meaning, timing parameter, and direction of authority.

The record is the referee

Both sides hold an identical immutable copy of every session. Neither party can rewrite history or dispute a timestamp.

Protocol over escape hatch

No early fallback to calls or messaging. Trust the protocol, train the users. The system enforces its own discipline.

3 Flash lifecycle — action signal logic ›

Flash 1 — normal

8-second full-screen. Amber glow. Single haptic burst synced to ripple rings. Standard delivery. No DND override.

Flash 2 — noticed

8-second full-screen again. Amber deepening. Longer haptic. Gap since flash 1 logged in session timeline. Receiver sees this is a follow-up.

Flash 3 — urgent

Red glow. Double pulse haptic. DP shakes on receiver screen. DND override fires if mutually enabled. Urgency communicated before brain processes text.

Flash 4 — critical

Crimson. Triple pulse. White jolt flash before crimson if DND override active. Session seals after this flash regardless. Sender locked. Cooldown begins.

Session close conditions

⚡ or ✔️ tap · ❌ tap · Signal expiry · Flash 4 no reply (critical seal) · Sender cancels before delivery. Each writes to history in session color.

New session after critical

Cooldown enforced by system. Next session opens neutral — amber, no carry-over. Old crimson session permanent in history. Never deletable.

5 Relationship setup flow core ›

Step 1 — Who

Search by name or @username. Or send a setup link — pre-fills proposed settings so receiver doesn't start blank. Link expires in 72 hours.

Step 2 — Signal type fork

Action or warmth. Everything downstream changes. Action = 5 steps. Warmth = 2 steps. Both can coexist between the same two people as separate relationships.

Step 3A — Roles and meanings (action)

Relationship type preset or custom. Superior and executor roles defined. Directional meaning set per side. Executor's return flash is always a question, never a command — enforced by UI label.

Step 3W — Shared meaning (warmth)

One meaning, both directions. No role labels. Palette color selection — purple, pink, teal, blue only. Amber and red unavailable — owned by action signals.

Step 4A — Session behaviour (action)

Signal expiry · Reply window · Cooldown after flash 4 · DND override threshold (mutual opt-in) · Max flashes per day. All per-relationship, not global.

Step 4W — Soft limits (warmth)

Daily soft limit (default 3). Fade without reply toggle. Soft history (count only). No expiry, cooldown, or escalation settings.

Step 5 — Confirm, send, negotiate

Full summary before sending. Receiver sees all settings with inline edit pills. Negotiate sends counter-proposal. Accept locks live. Both sides must agree same version.

6 Sender status screen interface ›

Live tab

Summary strip — got it / waiting / queued / critical counts. Active sessions with mini Orbs and submerged DPs. Dot cluster = flash count. Acknowledged today below active. Tap any row → session detail.

Session detail screen

Contact name and role. Signal intent. Full flash timeline with timestamps to the second. Gap between flashes in italic. Expiry bar live. Re-flash button or cooldown countdown.

History tab

Chronological, grouped by month. Color bar left of each entry. Tap to expand full session. Immutable. Both sides hold identical copies.

Received tab

Flashes sent to user as receiver. Their reply record. Immutable timestamps. Protects receiver from false accusations.

7 Relationship history view trust ›

Timeline tab

Full session history since inception. Grouped by month. Tap to expand. Each expanded view shows flash-by-flash breakdown with gap in italic. Seconds-level timestamps on all events.

Stats tab

Got it / declined / expired / critical totals. Response rate bar chart by month. Relationship health visible without editorial commentary.

Meanings tab

Current active meanings with effective dates. Full meaning change history with previous wording and change date. Neither party can dispute what the flash meant at any point in time.

Flash-back count

Private count of sessions that went unanswered and were marked ignored. Visible only to sender. Informational, not punitive. Tells sender: this person has a pattern.

Layer 1: dynamic glow — changes color per state, knows nothing about identity. Layer 2: frosted glass — backdrop blur creates submerged quality. Layer 3: identity ring — thin border inheriting state color. Layer 4: quiet DP — static, never animates, opacity and saturation only.

9 Contextual badges interface ›

Allowed

Location pin · ETA (5 / 10 / 30 min) · Floor or room number · Urgency flag · Time slot. Max 3 badge types per relationship. Configured at setup, not free-form.

Never allowed

Video · document · image · voice note · free text · link · emoji. Any attachment that says something new rather than sharpening the existing meaning is messaging. That line holds.

Interaction

Long-press flash button before sending → badge tray opens. One tap to attach. One badge per flash maximum. Badge appears alongside predefined message on receiver's full-screen takeover.
